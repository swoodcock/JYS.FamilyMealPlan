1
Tater Tot Casserole
Sunday dinner → Monday lunch · serves 4 + lunchbox
▾
Allergen-safeIron-richHidden vegLow-carb swap
Allergen-safe: The classic uses peas — swap to corn + green beans. Cream soup & cheese bake in = cooked dairy (no raw dairy). No egg anywhere.

Ingredients

  • 700 g lean ground beef iron
  • 1 onion (~150 g), diced · 2 carrots (~150 g), grated · 1 zucchini (~150 g), grated hidden veg
  • 60 g baby spinach, finely chopped iron + fiber
  • 300 g can cream of mushroom soup or roux (15 g flour + 360 ml milk)
  • 150 g corn + 150 g green beans, chopped no peas
  • 15 g (1 tbsp) tomato paste vit C
  • 450 g frozen tater tots · 100 g shredded cheddar
  • Salt, pepper, ½ tsp garlic powder

Method

  1. Brown beef with onion. Stir in carrot, zucchini, spinach; cook 3–4 min.
  2. Add tomato paste, corn, green beans, soup (or roux). Season. Spread in a baking dish.
  3. Top with tater tots, then cheese. Bake 200°C (400°F) 30–35 min until golden.
🔄 Low-carb: tots are high-GI. Plate a portion from the beef-veg layer + a scoop of mashed cauliflower instead of tots.
🩸 Iron-rich: beef + spinach + tomato paste. Serve with orange segments to boost iron absorption.
🥡 Lunchbox: reheats perfectly — microwave 60–90 s.

Mexican Lasagna
Monday dinner → Tuesday lunch · serves 4 + lunchbox
▾
Allergen-safeIron-rich ★Hidden vegLow-carb swapAdult spice
Allergen-safe: Skip raw sour cream on the allergen-safe portion. Melted cheese = fine. No egg/nuts. Black beans replace any peas.

Ingredients

  • 570 g ground beef (or half beef / half turkey) iron
  • 400 g can black beans, drained & rinsed iron + fiber ★
  • 1 onion (~150 g) + 1 bell pepper (~150 g), diced · 150 g corn hidden veg
  • 60 g baby spinach, chopped iron
  • 400 g can diced tomatoes + 240 g mild salsa vit C
  • 1 tbsp taco seasoning (mild), 1 tsp cumin
  • 8 corn or whole-grain tortillas · 150 g shredded Mexican-blend cheese
  • 1 avocado (for the low-carb portion) · Adults: jalapeños / hot sauce · optional sour cream (not the allergen-safe portion)

Method

  1. Brown beef + onion. Add pepper, corn, spinach, beans, tomatoes, salsa, spices. Simmer 8 min.
  2. Layer: sauce → tortillas → sauce → cheese, repeat; finish with cheese.
  3. Bake 190°C (375°F) 25 min. Rest 5 min before cutting.
🔄 Low-carb: beans stay (low-GI); tortillas are the load. Build a tortilla-free skillet portion over lettuce, or 1 low-carb tortilla + avocado.
🌶️ Adults: stir jalapeños/hot sauce into one half before layering, or add on top after plating the plain pieces.
🩸 Iron-rich ★: the week's iron star — beef + black beans + spinach + salsa vitamin C.
🥡 Lunchbox: a firm square reheats cleanly; no sour cream.
3
Chicken Alfredo "Tortellini"
Tuesday dinner → Wednesday lunch · serves 4 + lunchbox
▾
Allergen-safeHidden vegLow-carb swapAdult spice
Allergen-safe: Cheese tortellini pasta contains egg → for the egg-free portion use egg-free dried pasta (most dried penne/rotini is semolina + water — check the label). Alfredo is cooked cream/butter/parmesan (no raw dairy).

Ingredients

  • 400 g chicken breast (2), diced protein
  • Standard: ~300 g cheese tortellini · Egg-free portion: ~100 g egg-free dried penne/rotini
  • Alfredo: 30 g butter, 2 garlic cloves, 360 ml cream (or 360 ml milk + 15 g flour), 90 g grated parmesan
  • 250 g broccoli, finely chopped + 60 g spinach hidden veg + iron
  • Salt, pepper · Adults: red pepper flakes / chili crisp

Method

  1. Sear diced chicken until cooked; set aside. Boil pasta/tortellini; add broccoli for the last 3 min.
  2. Alfredo: melt butter, cook garlic 30 s, add cream, simmer, whisk in parmesan; stir in spinach until wilted.
  3. Toss chicken + pasta + broccoli in sauce.
🔄 Low-carb: serve the chicken + alfredo + broccoli over zucchini noodles or roasted broccoli — the sauce is low-carb.
🥦 Hidden veg: finely chopped broccoli + spinach vanish into the sauce.
🌶️ Adults: red pepper flakes or chili crisp on top after the plain bowls.
🥡 Lunchbox: add a teaspoon of milk when reheating so it loosens. Reheat 60 s, stir.

Hearty Bean Stew + Buns
Wednesday dinner → Thursday lunch · serves 4 + lunchbox
▾
Allergen-safeIron-rich ★High fiberLow-carbAdult spice
Allergen-safe: Naturally free of dairy, egg & nuts. No peas — kidney + cannellini beans. Check buns are egg-wash-free (milk in dough is fine — cooked).

Ingredients

  • 250 g ground or diced beef (optional, boosts iron) iron
  • 400 g can kidney beans + 400 g can cannellini/white beans iron + fiber ★
  • 1 onion (~150 g), 2 carrots (~150 g), 2 celery sticks (~120 g), 1 zucchini (~150 g), diced fiber
  • 1 small sweet potato (~250 g), diced fiber
  • 60 g kale or spinach, chopped iron
  • 400 g can diced tomatoes + 30 g tomato paste vit C
  • 750 ml low-sodium broth, 1 tsp smoked paprika, 1 bay leaf
  • Soft buns/dinner rolls · Adults: harissa, gochugaru, or hot sauce

Method

  1. Brown beef (if using) with onion. Add carrot, celery, sweet potato, zucchini; cook 5 min.
  2. Add tomatoes, paste, broth, beans, paprika, bay leaf. Simmer 25–30 min until sweet potato is tender.
  3. Stir in kale/spinach for the last 5 min. Serve with warm buns.
🔄 Low-carb: the stew is already low-GI. Skip the bun or use a low-carb roll; drizzle olive oil.
🩸 Iron-rich ★ + high fiber: beans, beef & kale for iron; carrot/celery/zucchini/sweet-potato add fiber. Pair with a vitamin-C side; go easy on milk at this meal.
🌶️ Adults: stir harissa or gochugaru into your bowls at the table.
🥡 Lunchbox: even better next day. Pack with a bun; reheat 90 s.
5
Meatloaf + Mashed Potatoes
Thursday dinner → Friday lunch · serves 4 + lunchbox
▾
Allergen-safeIron-richHidden vegLow-carb swapAdult spice
Allergen-safe: Standard meatloaf uses egg as binder → use a flax egg (10 g / 1 tbsp ground flax + 45 ml water, rest 5 min). Milk in the breadcrumbs cooks in (no raw dairy). No peas in the side.

Ingredients

  • 700 g ground beef iron
  • Binder: 80 g breadcrumbs (egg-free) + 80 ml milk + 1 flax egg
  • 1 grated carrot, ½ grated zucchini, 1 small onion, 60 g finely chopped spinach hidden veg + iron
  • Glaze: 60 g ketchup + 15 g tomato paste + splash Worcestershire
  • Mash: 900 g potatoes + butter + milk — plus 1 cauliflower head (~600 g, for the low-carb mash)
  • Side veg: carrots + 200 g green beans no peas
  • Adults: sriracha stirred into extra glaze

Method

  1. Mix beef, soaked breadcrumbs, flax egg, grated veg, spinach, salt, pepper. Press into a loaf pan.
  2. Brush with glaze. Bake 190°C (375°F) 45–55 min (internal 71°C / 160°F).
  3. Boil potatoes (and cauliflower separately for the low-carb mash); mash with butter + milk. Steam carrots + green beans.
🔄 Low-carb: swap mash for mashed cauliflower (or 50/50 blend). Make extra → it doubles as Sunday's tater-tot swap.
🩸 Iron-rich: beef + spinach hidden in the loaf. Serve with the tomato glaze (vit C) + green beans.
🌶️ Adults: brush your slices with sriracha-spiked glaze.
🥡 Lunchbox: a slice + a scoop of mash reheats great.

Bonus: Beef Bibimbap
Friday dinner (weekend, no lunch to match) · serves 4
▾
Allergen-safeIron-rich ★Loads of vegLow-carb swapAdult spiceDeconstructed
Allergen-safe: Traditionally topped with a fried egg → leave it off the egg-free portion. Everything else is safe. Serve it deconstructed: plain rice + plain beef + separate veg, no sauce.

Ingredients

  • 450 g thin-sliced beef (bulgogi cut) iron ★
  • Marinade: 30 ml soy sauce, 15 ml sesame oil, 2 garlic cloves, ¼ pear (grated), 1 tsp sugar
  • Veg: 100 g spinach, 1 carrot, 1 zucchini, 100 g mushrooms, 100 g bean sprouts — each lightly sautéed iron + fiber
  • Rice for the family · eggs for the standard bowls (leave off the egg-free portion)
  • Gochujang sauce on the side (adults + spice to taste)

Method

  1. Marinate beef 20 min, then sear hot & fast. Sauté each veg lightly with a pinch of salt + sesame.
  2. Build bowls: rice, beef, veg. Add fried egg + gochujang to the standard bowls. Plain plate: plain rice, plain beef, veg — no sauce, no egg.
🔄 Low-carb: swap white rice for cauliflower rice or a small scoop of quinoa; pile on veg + beef.
🩸 Iron-rich ★: beef + spinach + vitamin-C veg — a great iron night to close the week.
🥄 Plain option: keep it separate — plain protein + rice, veg on the side.

Sunday meal prep (~60–75 min)

  1. 10 min · 5 dinners
    Big veg chop. Dice all onions; grate carrots & zucchini; chop spinach. Split into labeled tubs: Casserole, Lasagna, Meatloaf, Stew. Wash broccoli + kale.
  2. 15 min · Sun + Mon
    Brown a big batch of beef (~1.3 kg) plain with onion. Split half → casserole, half → lasagna. Season at assembly. Cool & fridge.
  3. 10 min · low-carb swaps
    Mash a batch of cauliflower (both heads). Covers the casserole swap, lasagna side & Thursday mash swap.
  4. 8 min · Wed
    Stew base. Sauté stew onion/carrot/celery/sweet potato, then fridge. Wednesday = add beans, tomatoes, broth, simmer 25 min.
  5. 8 min · Thu
    Meatloaf mix. Make flax egg, combine beef + breadcrumbs + grated veg + spinach, press into loaf pan, cover, fridge. Thursday = glaze & bake.
  6. 10 min · Tue
    Alfredo + chicken. Dice & sear chicken; make alfredo, refrigerate (loosen with milk when reheating). Tuesday = boil pasta, toss.
  7. 4 min · labels
    Assembly-line the fridge front-to-back: Sun casserole → Mon lasagna → Tue chicken/alfredo → Wed stew → Thu meatloaf. Sticky-note the 1-line finish step on each.
  8. optional
    Bibimbap (Fri) needs no prep. If keen, mix the marinade & freeze the beef in it.

Weeknights become: Sun bake 35 m · Mon layer+bake 25 m · Tue boil+toss 15 m · Wed simmer 25 m · Thu glaze+bake 50 m (hands-off) · Fri quick stir-fry.
